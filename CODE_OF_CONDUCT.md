# Code of Conduct
## TacticalRelationshipManagement (TRM) Community Standards

> This Code of Conduct governs behaviour within the TRM contributor community.
> It does not govern behaviour outside of it. Frankly, based on the nature of
> this project, we are not in a position to govern behaviour outside of it.

---

## Our Pledge

We as contributors, maintainers, and users of TacticalRelationshipManagement pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, colour, religion, sexual orientation, or current number of active relationship nodes.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and technically rigorous community.

We pledge to maintain partition integrity within this contributor space. What happens in the repo stays in the repo. This is the one context in which we enforce consistency.

We acknowledge that several of us probably should not be trusted with this pledge given the nature of the project. We are making it anyway.

## Our Standards

### Examples of Behaviour That Contributes to a Positive Environment

- Using welcoming and inclusive language in issues, PRs, and comments
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and technical approaches
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism on your code
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project
- Showing empathy towards other community members who are, statistically, going through something
- Writing clear commit messages (see `CONTRIBUTING.md` for standards)
- Adding disclaimers when your contribution introduces new and creative forms of chaos
- Acknowledging when the Pizza Hut bug has affected you personally
- Reading the README before opening an issue
- Reading the README *again* before opening an iOS support issue

### Examples of Unacceptable Behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, or discriminatory language of any kind
- Personal attacks on contributors or maintainers
- Publishing others' private information without consent (we cannot stress the irony of this rule appearing in this document enough, but it stands)
- Submitting iOS support PRs after being explicitly told not to
- Submitting iOS support PRs disguised as something else
- Submitting iOS support PRs "as a joke" — we know. We've seen it. Close the branch.
- Defending the Pizza Hut alias as "working as intended" in a tone that implies you are not joking
- Opening issues titled "this doesn't work" with no further context
- Tagging maintainers in issues that have already been closed as `by-design`
- Claiming the geofence module "never worked" without providing device details, Android version, or minimum reproducible case
- Unsolicited advice about the ethical dimensions of this project — we have thirty disclaimers, we are aware
- Asking maintainers for personal advice related to active use of the software — we are not relationship counsellors (disclaimer #14)
- Any behaviour that would constitute a "collision event" within the contributor community (defined below)

## Definitions

### Collision Event (Community Context)

A **Collision Event** within the TRM contributor community occurs when:

1. Two contributors who are using TRM in a personal capacity become aware of each other's existence through this repository
2. A contributor's partner discovers this repository via the contributor's GitHub activity and opens an issue
3. A contributor recognises a bug report as describing their own personal situation and responds in a way that reveals this

Collision Events within the contributor community are handled under the **Graceful Degradation Protocol** (see Enforcement section).

### Partition Integrity

**Partition integrity** is maintained when contributor interactions within this space remain professional, technically focused, and do not result in the propagation of personal information across community nodes.

All contributors are expected to maintain partition integrity at all times.

The irony of a cheating-adjacent software project having strict information boundaries is not lost on us. It is, in fact, the point.

### The Pizza Hut Condition

A contributor is said to be **in the Pizza Hut Condition** when they have submitted a bug report, issue, or PR comment that inadvertently reveals more about their personal use of the software than they intended.

Contributors in the Pizza Hut Condition will be treated with dignity and discretion. We will not acknowledge what we have inferred. We will simply close the issue and move on. This is a safe space. Conditionally.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the project maintainers. All reports will be reviewed promptly and investigated fairly.

Maintainers are obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of any incident. This confidentiality will be maintained more reliably than any other form of confidentiality documented in this repository.

### Enforcement Tiers

**Tier 1: The Correction**

*Trigger:* Minor violations. First-time offences. Tone issues. Gentle iOS attempts.

*Response:* A private message from a maintainer explaining the issue. No public record. Think of it as a warning shot across the bow.

*Example response:* "Hey, we noticed your PR adds a `CallKit` import. We appreciate the effort but iOS support is not on the roadmap. Please see the manifesto. We've closed the branch."

---

**Tier 2: The Warning**

*Trigger:* Repeated minor violations. Second iOS attempt. Defending the Pizza Hut bug aggressively. Opening a closed issue without new information.

*Response:* A formal warning with documented consequences. Continued violation results in temporary ban.

*Example response:* "This is your second iOS PR this month. We admire the persistence. We do not share it. This has been logged."

---

**Tier 3: The Temporary Ban**

*Trigger:* Serious violations. Harassment. Third iOS attempt (at this point it is harassment). Doxxing a contributor. Publishing another contributor's personal use of the software.

*Response:* Temporary ban from all community interaction. Duration determined by maintainers based on severity.

*This tier is called internally: **Ghost Mode**.*

---

**Tier 4: The Permanent Ban**

*Trigger:* Sustained pattern of harmful behaviour. Harassment after reinstatement. Actual malicious conduct.

*Response:* Permanent removal from the community.

*This tier is called internally: **The Geofence Didn't Save Them**.*

### The Graceful Degradation Protocol

In the event of a **Collision Event** within the contributor community, maintainers will invoke the Graceful Degradation Protocol:

1. All parties are privately contacted
2. The relevant issues, PRs, or comments are quietly closed
3. No public record of the collision is maintained
4. All parties are reminded that this is a satirical software repository and perhaps there are more productive ways to process what has happened
5. A new disclaimer is added to the README

The Graceful Degradation Protocol exists because we recognise that the nature of this project may, in rare and unfortunate cases, result in contributors recognising each other in ways they did not anticipate.

We did not design the software for this use case. We are not sure we designed it for any use case. See the disclaimers.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behaviour. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behaviour they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers also reserve the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers will recuse themselves from enforcement decisions if they are personally involved in the incident, have a conflict of interest, or are currently in the Pizza Hut Condition themselves.
